Learning Style Test

Audiory

Introduction

Auditory learners lean by hearing and listening. It means that they can understand and remember things that they have heard. They store information by the way it sounds and they have an easier time understanding spoken instructions than written ones. They often learn by reading out loud because they have to hear it or speak it in order to know it. Auditory learners often hum or talk to themselves or others when they become bored. Many people may think that they are not paying attention, even though they may be hearing and understanding everything that is being said.

Meaning for Me

For me, the auditory learning style means that to understand something, it needs to be heard. This means that attending lectures is benefitcial and the physcial sound of explaining may help solve any issues I have with not understanding something. It also means that I must seek physical help through face-to-face meetings rather than through email or non-verbal contact methods.

Influence Behaviour in a Team

I believe that it may influence my behaviour in the team as I am more likely to request that the other team members verbally express what they are doing and how they are changing specific items in the project or assignment. It will also mean that it may require more time for face-to-face meetups rather than over the internet texting and at-home working. This would require more effort from everyone in the team.

Big Five Personality Test

5 Factor Personality Test

Introduction

The big five personality traits are the best accepted and most commonly used model of personality in academic psychology. The big five come from the statistical study of responses to personality items. Using a technique called factor analysis researchers can look at the responses of people to hundreds of personality items and ask the question "what is the best was to summarize an individual?". This has been done with many samples from all over the world and the general result is that, while there seem to be unlimited personality variables, five stand out from the pack in terms of explaining a lot of a persons answers to questions about their personality: extraversion, neuroticism, agreeableness, conscientiousness and openness to experience. The big-five are not associated with any particular test, a variety of measures have been developed to measure them. This test uses the Big-Five Factor Markers from the International Personality Item Pool, developed by Goldberg (1992).
